Superscript & Subscrip

Superscripts are characters set above the normal line of type (e.g., in 2ⁿᵈ) and subscripts are characters set below (e.g., in Cᵥₑₓ). There are many reasons to use them in charts — for example, in footnotes or for chemical and physical formulas. Or maybe you just find them pretty!


When writing a chemical formula, such as H20, the number of atoms of a given element within the described molecule is represented using a subscripted number; in the case of water, the subscripted "2" indicates that there are two atoms of hydrogen in the molecule. Almost every developer's favorite molecule is C8H10N4O2, also known as "caffeine."
